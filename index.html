Protecting Our Oceanic Heritage

Overview of Marine Biodiversity in Pakistan

The marine life in Pakistan is both rich and fragile. With over 1000 species recorded, the Arabian Sea supports coral reefs, sea turtles, endangered dolphins, and mangrove ecosystems.

Key Marine Hotspots

  • Churna Island: Popular for coral reefs and scuba diving.
  • Sandspit Beach: Vital for sea turtle nesting.
  • Sonmiani Bay & Jiwani: Critical coastal wetlands for biodiversity.

Threats to Marine Ecosystems

Pakistan's marine ecosystems face numerous threats including oil pollution in Karachi, overfishing in the Arabian Sea, and ghost nets that trap marine animals. Rising sea temperatures due to global warming are worsening these issues.

Conservation Challenges

Despite initiatives by NGOs like WWF Pakistan and Marine Conservation Pakistan, a lack of marine protected areas and enforcement remain challenges.

Conservation Efforts & Organizations

Several organizations are actively involved in marine conservation including:

  • Marine Conservation Pakistan: Works to establish awareness and protected zones.
  • WWF Pakistan: Monitors endangered species and pollution impact.

How You Can Help

You can volunteer with local NGOs, avoid plastic waste, and participate in eco-tours to Churna Island to learn more.
